Chapter 3: Kerajaan Proselytism

Elite Conversion to Islam

  • Political & Economic Incentives: Access to Arab/Asian trade networks and global Islamic legitimacy.
  • Symbolic Power: Titles like Zillullah fil-Alam ("God's Shadow on Earth") and hybrid royal designations.
  • Countering Christianity: Alliances against Portuguese/Spanish colonialism through religious solidarity.

Subtle & Assertive Strategies

Subtle Strategies (Halus)

  • Royal ceremonies and genealogies tracing to Alexander the Great
  • Intermarriage (e.g., Melaka-Pasai) and Orang Laut integration
  • Cultural fusion:
    "Adat bersendi hukum, Hukum bersendi kitabullah"

Assertive Strategies (Regas)

  • Hybrid legal codes (e.g., Undang-Undang Melaka)
  • Military conquests using Ottoman/Chinese technology
  • Scholarly networks: Sufi missionaries and pondok schools

A Tale of Three Kerajaans

Melaka: The Islamic Archetype

15th-century trade hub with 84 languages spoken. Fell to Portuguese in 1511 but inspired Islamic revival in Aceh/Johor.

Legacy

Through elite conversion and strategic adaptation, the kerajaans forged a resilient Malay-Muslim identity that withstood colonial pressures.
